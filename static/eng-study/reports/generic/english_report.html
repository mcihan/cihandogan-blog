6 · The "Yeah-yeah-yeah" Problem

Why this matters

You said "yeah" 796 times in 11 lessons. That's 72 yeahs per lesson, or one every 30 seconds. Even worse, you said "yeah yeah yeah" (three in a row) 112 times.

In English, repeating a confirmation 3 times signals one of two things to a native listener:

  • Anxiety — like you're trying to reassure them you're listening
  • Politeness fatigue — like you want to interrupt but can't

Native speakers use varied acknowledgments:

SituationWhat to say
You agree"Right." / "Exactly." / "Makes sense."
You understand"Got it." / "I see." / "Mm-hm."
You're surprised"Really?" / "Wow." / "Is that so?"
You agree strongly"Absolutely." / "Totally." / "100%."
You're thinking(silence + nod) — don't fill it
